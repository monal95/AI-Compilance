[SECTION: LEGAL METROLOGY ACT, 2009]

[Section 18 - Pre-packaged Commodities]
No person shall manufacture, pack, sell, import, distribute, deliver, offer, expose or possess for sale any pre-packaged commodity unless such package is in such standard quantities or number and bears thereon such declarations and particulars in such manner as may be prescribed.

[Section 36 - Penalties]
Whoever manufactures, packs, imports, sells, distributes or delivers any pre-packaged commodity which does not conform to the declarations on the package shall be punished with fine which may extend to twenty-five thousand rupees for the first offence and fifty thousand rupees for the second and subsequent offences.

[SECTION: LEGAL METROLOGY (PACKAGED COMMODITIES) RULES, 2011]

[Rule 6 - Mandatory Declarations on Pre-packaged Commodities]
(1) Every package shall bear thereon or on a label securely affixed thereto, a definite, plain and conspicuous declaration made in such manner as may be prescribed, giving:
    (a) the name and address of the manufacturer, or where the manufacturer is not the packer, the name and address of the manufacturer and packer;
    (b) the common or generic name of the commodity contained in the package;
    (c) the net quantity, in terms of standard unit of weight or measure, of the commodity contained in the package;
    (d) the month and year in which the commodity is manufactured or pre-packed or imported;
    (e) the retail sale price of the package (MRP);
    (f) the consumer care details including the address for consumer complaints.

(2) Where the manufacturer is not the packer, the package shall also bear the name and address of the packer.

(3) In case of imported packages, the name and address of the importer shall also be declared on the package along with the country of origin.

[Rule 7 - Common or Generic Name]
The common or generic name of the commodity shall be printed or inscribed legibly and prominently on the package. Trade name alone shall not be used as a substitute for the common or generic name.

[Rule 9 - Declaration of Net Quantity]
(1) The declaration of quantity shall be expressed in terms of:
    - Weight: gram (g) or kilogram (kg)
    - Volume: millilitre (ml) or litre (L)
    - Length: centimetre (cm) or metre (m)
    - Area: square centimetre (sq cm) or square metre (sq m)
    - Number: units or pieces

(2) The following expressions are PROHIBITED in net quantity declaration:
    - "approximately" or "approx"
    - "about"
    - "minimum" or "min"
    - "average" or "avg"
    - "not less than"
    - "at least" or "atleast"
    - Any qualifying words that suggest approximate quantity

(3) The net quantity shall be accurate and the actual quantity shall not be less than the declared quantity minus the maximum permissible error.

[Rule 10 - Retail Sale Price (MRP)]
(1) The retail sale price shall be declared in the following format:
    "MRP Rs. [amount] (inclusive of all taxes)" or "M.R.P. ₹ [amount] (incl. of all taxes)"

(2) The price shall include all taxes - GST, CGST, SGST, IGST, etc.

(3) Rounding Rules for MRP:
    - Paise less than 50: Round down to nearest rupee
    - Paise 50 to 95: Round to 50 paise

(4) MRP shall be printed clearly and legibly on the principal display panel.

[Rule 18 - Prohibition of Sale Above MRP]
No retail dealer or other person including manufacturer, packer, importer and wholesale dealer shall make any sale of any commodity in packed form at a price exceeding the retail sale price thereof. This applies to all channels including e-commerce platforms.

[Rule 11 - Principal Display Panel]
(1) All mandatory declarations shall be made on the principal display panel of the package.
(2) The declarations shall be legible and conspicuous.
(3) Minimum font height requirements based on package area:
    - Up to 100 sq cm: 1 mm
    - 100-500 sq cm: 2 mm
    - 500-2500 sq cm: 4 mm
    - Above 2500 sq cm: 6 mm

[SECTION: MAXIMUM PERMISSIBLE ERROR (SCHEDULE II)]

[For Weight and Volume:]
| Quantity Range          | Maximum Permissible Error |
|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Up to 50 g/ml          | 9% (but not exceeding 4.5 g/ml) |
| 50 to 100 g/ml         | 4.5 g/ml |
| 100 to 200 g/ml        | 4.5% |
| 200 to 300 g/ml        | 9 g/ml |
| 300 to 500 g/ml        | 3% |
| 500 to 1000 g/ml       | 15 g/ml |
| 1000 to 10000 g/ml     | 1.5% |
| 10000 to 15000 g/ml    | 150 g/ml |
| Above 15000 g/ml       | 1% |

[For Length:]
| Quantity Range         | Maximum Permissible Error |
|-----------------------|---------------------------|
| Up to 10 m            | 2% |
| Above 10 m            | 1% |

[For Area:]
| Quantity Range         | Maximum Permissible Error |
|-----------------------|---------------------------|
| Up to 10 sq m         | 4% |
| Above 10 sq m         | 1% |

[For Number/Count:]
Maximum 2% error in the declared count.

[SECTION: WHOLESALE PACKAGES - Rule 26]
Every wholesale package shall contain:
(1) Name and address of the manufacturer/packer
(2) Generic name of the commodity
(3) Total number of retail packages contained
(4) Net quantity of each retail package
(5) Month/year of manufacture

[SECTION: DECEPTIVE PACKAGING - Rule 4]
(1) A package shall not be so sized or shaped or made so as to mislead or deceive the consumer.
(2) Excessive headspace or void space beyond reasonable requirements is prohibited.
(3) Package size must be reasonably related to the quantity of contents.
(4) Interior space void over 25% without structural necessity may be considered deceptive.

[SECTION: ADVERTISEMENT RULES - Rule 21]
(1) No advertisement shall contain any misleading information about the quantity.
(2) If MRP is displayed in an advertisement, the net quantity must also be prominently displayed.
(3) Selling price in advertisements cannot exceed the MRP.

[SECTION: REGISTRATION - Rule 27]
(1) Every manufacturer, packer, and importer of packaged commodities must be registered with the appropriate authority.
(2) Registration number format varies by state and issuing authority.
(3) Registration ensures traceability and accountability.

[SECTION: EXEMPTIONS - Rule 3]
The following are EXEMPT from these rules:
(1) Packages containing quantity less than 10 grams or 10 millilitres
(2) Fast food/restaurant food sold immediately after preparation
(3) Agricultural produce in packages exceeding 50 kg
(4) Certain institutional packages (not for retail sale)

[SECTION: PENALTIES]

[General Violations:]
- First offence: Fine up to ₹2,000
- Second and subsequent offences: Fine up to ₹4,000

[Registration Violations:]
- Operating without registration: ₹4,000 fine

[Selling Above MRP:]
- Fine as per Section 36 of the Act
- May extend to ₹25,000 for first offence
- Up to ₹50,000 for subsequent offences

[SECTION: E-COMMERCE APPLICABILITY]
All rules applicable to physical retail packages also apply to:
(1) Product listings on e-commerce platforms
(2) Product images must clearly show all mandatory declarations
(3) Product description must include all mandatory information
(4) Selling price cannot exceed MRP
